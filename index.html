On Writing
A few notes on craft and clarity

The Case for Clarity

Good writing is not about impressing the reader — it is about respecting them. Every unnecessary word is a small act of disrespect: it costs their time and dilutes your meaning.

The most valuable of all talents is that of never using two words when one will do.
— Thomas Jefferson

When you strip a sentence to its bones, what remains should stand on its own. A sentence that relies on its decoration is one that does not trust itself.

The First Draft

Write fast. Edit slow. The first draft is not supposed to be good — it is supposed to exist. You cannot revise a blank page. Write without judgment, then judge without mercy.
